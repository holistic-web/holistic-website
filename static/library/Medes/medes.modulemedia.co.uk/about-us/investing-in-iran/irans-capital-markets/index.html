Investing In Iran

Iran's stock market is one of the largest and most liquid amongst all Frontier Markets.

Size and Liquidity

Iran’s stock market is one of the largest and most liquid amongst all Frontier Markets. The Iranian equity markets have an average daily liquidity of $150m, not including block trading, with negligible foreign participation.
Negotiations are in progress for index inclusion for Iran. If Iran was included in MSCI Frontier Index then we estimate it could constitute 25% to 32% of the index, based on the liquidity and size of the stock market (source: Renaissance Capital).

600+ Stocks Across 2 Exchanges with Strong Diversification

Iran's financial markets are well diversified across a wide range of major sectors.
More than 600+ stocks are listed on Iran's 2 exchanges, presenting a diverse set of industries and market cap.

A solid regulatory framework and transparent access to data

Strong Regulatory Oversight

  • SEO, the regulator, oversees the exchanges, market regulations as well as service providers bringing a comprehensive and thorough approach
  • FIPPA (Foreign Investor Promotion and Protection Act) License. This licence provides Medes a range of measures protecting the company against expropriation and adverse government actions
  • Custody is handled by the Central Securities Depositary, ensuring minimization of counterparty risk
  • The exchange was founded in 1967

Transparent

  • Companies in the financial sector and issuers with a market cap above $200m release IFRS accounts
  • Extensive disclosure on management guidance. Level of details much higher than in other mainstream market
  • Live transparent access to online trading and data
  • Full access as to holdings held in the centralised depository
A large, diverse and liquid market within a strong operational and regulatory framework

A resilient track record

Despite an ever changing political and economic environment, the TSE has compounded 16% (in USD terms) on average over the past 20 years.
